### Formatting Tips for ATS Compatibility

1.  **Standard Fonts:** Use common, easily readable fonts like Arial, Calibri, Times New Roman, or Georgia. Avoid overly stylized or script fonts.
2.  **Clear Section Headings:** Use standard headings like "Professional Experience," "Education," "Skills," "Projects." Ensure they are distinct and easily parsed.
3.  **No Tables or Columns:** Many ATS systems struggle to read text within tables or multi-column layouts. Stick to a single-column format.
4.  **Standard File Format:** Save your resume as a `.docx` or `.pdf` file. PDFs are generally preferred for preserving formatting, but ensure it's text-based (not an image scan).
5.  **Avoid Headers/Footers:** Information placed in the header or footer might be stripped by some ATS. Keep contact information within the main body.
6.  **Keyword Density:** Naturally integrate keywords from the job description throughout your resume, especially in the summary, experience descriptions, and skills section. Don't just list them; use them in context.
7.  **Chronological Order:** List your work experience in reverse chronological order (most recent first).
8.  **No Graphics or Images:** Avoid logos, photos, or complex graphics, as ATS cannot interpret them.
